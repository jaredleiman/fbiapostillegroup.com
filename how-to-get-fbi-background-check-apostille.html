How to Get an FBI Background Check Apostille

FBI Apostille Group April 2026 10 min read
The 5-step FBI apostille process — FBI Apostille Group

The FBI apostille process can seem intimidating at first — it involves federal agencies, fingerprints, and authentication certificates most people have never heard of. But the process is actually straightforward once you understand each stage.

This guide walks you through exactly how to get an FBI background check apostille, from the moment you decide you need one to the day your authenticated document arrives at your door.

Before You Start

Know your destination country before you begin. Different countries have different requirements for FBI apostilles — some require certified translations, some need specific formatting, and some are not Hague Convention members and require a different process altogether. We'll advise you on your country's specific requirements when you submit your order.

The 5-Step FBI Apostille Process

1

2

Choose Your Submission Method

You have two options: submit existing FBI documents digitally as a PDF, or receive a fingerprint card by mail to get printed at a local police station, UPS Store, or authorized fingerprinting location near you. We send detailed instructions for whichever method you choose. If your fingerprints are rejected by the FBI, we resend a card at no extra charge.

Days 2–7
3

FBI Identity History Summary Processing

We submit your materials to the FBI's Identity History Summary division. The FBI searches their fingerprint and criminal records databases and generates your official Identity History Summary. This is the longest stage of the process — standard submissions take 6–8 weeks, while expedited priority positioning can reduce this to 3–4 weeks. We track your application and notify you when results are received.

Weeks 2–8 (standard) / Weeks 2–4 (expedited)
4

U.S. Department of State Apostille Certification

Once we receive your FBI results, we immediately submit them to the U.S. Department of State. The Department affixes the official apostille certificate — a separate document attached to your FBI results that certifies it as a genuine U.S. government record, recognized under the Hague Convention. This stage typically takes 1–2 weeks.

Weeks 9–10 (standard) / Weeks 5 (expedited)
5

Secure Delivery

Your completed, authenticated FBI apostille document is shipped directly to you. Standard orders ship via USPS with tracking. Expedited orders ship via FedEx Priority for faster delivery. You receive tracking information so you can monitor delivery in real time.

2–5 business days after apostille is issued

What Documents Do You Need?

The documents required depend on your submission method:

If Submitting by Fingerprint Card (Mail-In)

  • Completed FBI fingerprint card (we send this to you)
  • A completed personal information form (we provide this)
  • Copy of your government-issued photo ID

If Submitting Existing Documents (Digital PDF)

  • Your existing FBI Identity History Summary (PDF)
  • Confirmation that the document was issued within the required timeframe (most countries require a document issued within 6–12 months)
Document Age Requirements

Many foreign governments require that your FBI background check be issued within a specific timeframe — commonly 3, 6, or 12 months before your application. If your existing FBI results are older than this, you will need to request a new one via fingerprint submission. We'll advise you on your destination country's specific requirements.

Common Questions

How do I get fingerprinted for an FBI apostille?

We mail you an FBI-compliant fingerprint card with detailed instructions. You take the card to a local police station, sheriff's office, or in many areas a UPS Store or authorized fingerprinting service. They print your fingers onto the card, and you mail it back to us. The process usually takes less than 30 minutes.

Can I submit a PDF of an old FBI background check?

Yes, if your existing FBI results are recent enough to meet your destination country's requirements. Many countries require the document to be issued within 6–12 months. We'll advise you on whether your existing document qualifies or whether you need a new one.

What happens after I receive my apostille?

You submit your apostilled FBI background check as part of your visa, immigration, citizenship, adoption, or employment application to the relevant foreign authority. Some countries also require a certified translation of the document — we can coordinate translation services as an add-on if needed.
