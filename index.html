What is an FBI apostille and why do I need one? +
An FBI apostille is an official authentication of your FBI background check results by the U.S. Department of State. Required by over 100 Hague Convention countries for immigration, visa applications, international employment, foreign adoption, dual citizenship, and more. Without it, foreign authorities will not accept your FBI background check as a valid government document.
How long does the FBI apostille process take? +
Standard processing takes approximately 8 to 10 weeks. Expedited service takes approximately 3 to 5 weeks. These are averages based on typical federal agency processing times and are subject to change. Timelines are set by the FBI and U.S. Department of State and cannot be shortened beyond their official limits.
Which countries require an FBI background check apostille? +
Over 100 Hague Convention countries accept FBI apostilles including Italy, Spain, Germany, France, Mexico, Brazil, Colombia, Australia, Japan, South Korea, the UAE, and many others. Countries not party to the Hague Convention require a different process called legalization — contact us and we will advise you.
Can I submit documents as a PDF or does it have to be by mail? +
Both options are available. You can submit existing documents digitally as a PDF, or we can mail you a fingerprint card for in-person fingerprinting if a new FBI Identity History Summary is required. We will advise you on which method applies to your specific situation when you place your order.
How much does an FBI apostille service cost? +
Our standard FBI apostille service starts at $275, including our service fee, all submission coordination, and return shipping. Expedited service is $325. Separate FBI and Department of State government fees are fully itemized before payment with no hidden charges.
Can I get an FBI apostille if I have a criminal record? +
Yes. The FBI issues Identity History Summary results regardless of criminal record. The apostille authenticates the document — it does not change or conceal any record content. Whether a record affects your visa or immigration application depends on the destination country and application type.

About FBI Background Check Apostilles

What Is an FBI Background Check Apostille?

An FBI background check apostille is a two-step authentication process. First, you request your FBI Identity History Summary — an official federal record of any criminal history associated with your fingerprints. Second, the U.S. Department of State affixes an apostille certificate confirming it is a genuine U.S. government record recognized internationally.

This makes your FBI background check legally recognized in any of the 120+ countries party to the Hague Convention of 1961. Without the apostille, foreign governments, employers, universities, and adoption agencies will not accept your FBI results as authentic.

Why Use a Professional FBI Apostille Service?

While possible to request an FBI apostille yourself, the process involves navigating multiple federal agencies, using specific forms, meeting exact formatting requirements, and mailing to precise addresses. Errors result in rejections that add weeks or months to your timeline.

FBI Apostille Group has processed hundreds of applications for clients across the United States and internationally. We accept both mail-in fingerprint submissions and digital PDF documents, know exactly what each country requires, and track applications through both the FBI and Department of State efficiently.

FBI Apostille for Immigration to Italy, Spain & Europe

Italy, Spain, Germany, France, and most EU countries require an FBI apostille as part of the long-stay visa or residency application process. Italy's Elective Residency Visa and Digital Nomad Visa programs specifically list an apostilled FBI background check as a mandatory document. Our team prepares FBI apostilles that meet European immigration authority requirements precisely.

FBI Apostille for Latin America: Mexico, Brazil & Colombia

Mexico, Brazil, Colombia, and most of Latin America are Hague Convention signatories requiring apostilled FBI background checks for immigration, work permits, and residency. Requirements vary — Brazil requires certified Portuguese translation alongside the apostille, while Mexico and Colombia typically accept the English original with apostille. We handle all country-specific requirements.
